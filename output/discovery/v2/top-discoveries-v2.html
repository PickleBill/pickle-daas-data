7. What We Don't Know Yet

  • Multi-venue distribution: All clips likely from one venue. We can't distinguish venue effects from sport-wide patterns.
  • Brand detection reliability: 20% reproducibility means brand frequency counts are approximate at best.
  • DUPR prediction validity: 0% agreement in verification. The model defaults to "3.5-4.0" — this is prompt bias, not prediction.
  • Temporal trends: Data spans 4 months across 2 years (Apr 2024, May 2024, Jul 2024, Apr 2026). Gaps make trend analysis unreliable.
  • Player identity: No player tracking across clips. The "494 players detected" may be 20 actual people.
  • Ground truth: No human-labeled validation set. All quality metrics are AI-estimated.

10. Next Steps to Improve Data Quality

  1. Multi-venue data: Get clips from Peak, Chicken N Pickle, or any non-Lifetime venue. Even 50 clips would unlock venue comparison.
  2. Prompt engineering: Brand detection at 20% needs the most work. Constrain Gemini's brand list, add few-shot examples.
  3. Human validation set: Label 50 clips by hand (brands, skill, shots). Measure AI accuracy against ground truth.
  4. DUPR calibration: Get 20 clips from players with known DUPR ratings. Measure prediction accuracy.
  5. Sequential sampling: Run on non-highlight clips (raw court footage) to compare against highlight-selected data.

In V1, "Courtana clips avg 7.43/10" referred to clips where the Courtana brand was detected on screen (logo on court/screen). This is the venue's own camera system branding, NOT a product category. Clips with Courtana branding visible may correlate with better-positioned cameras, explaining higher quality scores.
