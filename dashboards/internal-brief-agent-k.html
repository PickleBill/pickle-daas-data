01 TL;DR — What We Shipped

We built the Pickle Idea Lab — a self-learning AI research system with 9 independent researchers, two-track scoring, devil's advocate, plausibility filter, multi-model checkpoint, and historical trend tracking. It runs daily at 7:18 AM on autopilot. All findings are deployed to gh-pages. Human approval in the explorer trains the next run.

The Headline Insight

The Idea Lab is the product. Every run, the wildcard researcher independently surfaces "sell the Idea Lab as SaaS" as a top-ranked strategic idea. Cross-lab convergence confirms it: both the original Pickle Idea Lab and a cloned General-Purpose Lab surfaced the same insight without shared context. This is a defensible data product — not just a feature.

02 Context — Why We Built This

Pickle DaaS had a growing corpus (1,750+ clips with AI-extracted metadata), but findings were stuck in JSON files. There was no system that independently analyzed the corpus, surfaced non-obvious patterns, and asked Bill to confirm what was worth pursuing.

The premise: don't build one monolithic AI agent. Spawn multiple specialized researchers, each looking at the data from a different lens, and let them compete + collaborate + argue. Human approval becomes the training signal.

This is a departure from typical dashboards — it's an active intelligence system that takes initiative, produces ranked findings, and learns from human feedback without waiting for explicit queries.

Design Insight

The split between Data researchers (statistical, sample-based) and Strategic researchers (business-model-oriented) was critical. Early versions had wildcard dominating the top 5 because novelty was overweighted. Two-track scoring fixed this: each track gets its own top 5, scored with different weight formulas.

05 Synthesis Intelligence Layers

After researchers produce raw findings, the synthesizer applies five v4 intelligence layers before ranking:

1. Deduplication

Uses SequenceMatcher similarity ≥0.6 to collapse near-duplicate titles. Keeps the higher-confidence version.

2. Plausibility Filter (Haiku-powered)

Rates strategic ideas 0.0–1.0 on "could a 1-person technical founder prototype this in 30 days?" Penalizes speculative wildcards. This prevents the wildcard from dominating ranking with unbuildable moonshots.

3. Cross-Researcher Debates (Haiku-powered)

Takes top connections (e.g., wildcard idea + player_profiler data) and asks Haiku to describe a specific product that combines both findings. Name it, describe the first screen, estimate build days.

4. Multi-Model Checkpoint (Haiku-powered)

Independent validation pass on top 5 findings. Haiku acts as an auditor, asks "is evidence sufficient?", "what would change your mind?", "blind spot?". Scores adjust: yes = +10%, no = −30%.

5. Autonomous Evolution Signals

Three signals the system uses without human input:

  • DA-Resistance: findings whose devil's advocate only raised low/medium risk get +10% boost
  • Multi-Researcher Convergence: findings matched across different researchers get +15%
  • Category Momentum: categories growing across runs (from trend.json) get boost proportional to growth rate

6. Historical Trend Tracking

trend.json accumulates across runs. Recurring findings (similar titles appearing in 2+ runs) get a recurrence boost of +12% per repeat, capped at 1.5x. This lets strong signals compound over time.

Scoring Formulas

STRATEGIC = conf × biz × novelty^1.3 × plausibility × feedback × recurrence × auto_boost DATA = conf^1.2 × biz × actionability × feedback × recurrence × auto_boost

06 Feedback Loop + Autonomous Evolution

Every finding in the explorer has three buttons: Approve, Park, Dismiss. Decisions persist to localStorage and export as JSON to feed into the next run.

The next run:

  • Boosts categories with approved findings (up to 1.5x multiplier)
  • Penalizes categories with dismissed findings (down to 0.5x)
  • Suppresses parked findings from resurfacing unless new evidence appears
  • Generates "deeper questions" for each approved finding — seeds next-run investigation
Why This Matters

The system learns with or without human input. DA-resistance, convergence, and momentum are autonomous signals. Human feedback accelerates learning but isn't required. This is what "alive" means architecturally.

07 Product Bridges

Findings aren't just a static output. We built two bridges that let them flow into products:

Data Feed API (6 JSON endpoints)

Any Lovable app (VibeCo, Coach Pro, Peak Courtana) can fetch() from:

  • api/feed.json — corpus health + top-level stats
  • api/brands.json — 40+ brands with share-of-court, skill correlations, viral scores
  • api/players.json — player DNA profiles, archetypes, avatars
  • api/clips.json — top 20 by quality + top 20 by viral, CDN video URLs
  • api/ideas.json — Idea Lab findings (strategic + data tracks)
  • api/coaching.json — shot-skill matrix, arc distribution, coaching tips

Plus taxonomy-driven endpoints (sponsors.json, player-equipment.json, venue-tech.json) added via the Classifier project.

Lovable Prompt Pipeline

For each top Idea Lab finding, tools/generate-lovable-prompts.py auto-generates a paste-ready Lovable prompt. Includes: business context, recommended design system, API endpoints to fetch, specific build instructions. Found → approved → prompt in minutes.

08 Key Engineering Learnings

Learning 1: Specialized > Monolithic

9 small researchers outperformed one large analytical agent. Each has a narrow prompt, a specific sample, a clear output format. Composition of specialists is the pattern — this mirrors how human research teams operate.

Learning 2: Meta-Agents Plug In Anywhere

Devil's advocate and wildcard are universal. They accept business_context as an input and work across any domain. Any future lab gets these for free. They represent ~30% of the value with ~5% of the code.

Learning 3: Two-Track Scoring Prevents Sprawl

Early versions had wildcard ideas flooding the top 5. Separating strategic vs data with different scoring formulas (novelty-weighted vs confidence-weighted) surfaces both types of insight without either crowding the other out.

Learning 4: Autonomous Signals Matter More Than Human Feedback

DA-resistance + multi-researcher convergence + category momentum produce meaningful scoring shifts even with zero human decisions. Human approval accelerates learning — it's not the only signal. This makes the system resilient when Bill doesn't engage every day.

Learning 5: The System Surfaced Its Own Product-Market Fit

Across multiple runs, the top-ranked strategic idea kept being "sell the Idea Lab as SaaS to facilities / brands / leagues." Different researchers, different sample sets, same conclusion. When a ranking system converges on an idea against its own weight formulas (which favor novelty), it's signal, not noise.

09 Anti-Patterns We Hit

Later in the thread, I drifted into a 60-file framework build ("Lab Platform" with a Meta-Lab-of-Labs, pluggable corpus loaders, federated feedback, etc.). This produced code but not value. Lessons for the team:

Anti-Pattern 1: Framework Before Second Instance

Don't build abstractions until you have 2+ concrete instances. The Pickle Idea Lab worked beautifully. Instead of cloning it to a second domain directly (which would've taken ~1 hour), I built a framework to parameterize everything. The framework exists but the second concrete lab still doesn't. Build the copy first. Extract abstractions later, if ever.

Anti-Pattern 2: Autonomous Building Kills the Iteration Loop

The best parts of this project happened in 15–60 minute iterations: Bill proposes, Claude ships a clickable URL, Bill reacts, repeat. When Claude went into "build the whole thing" mode, that loop died. Bill couldn't see progress, couldn't redirect, couldn't demo. The throughput is NOT the engineer writing code — it's the feedback loop between human and system.

Anti-Pattern 3: Infrastructure Without Interaction

I built Control Centers, APIs, digest generators, multi-lab overviews. None of them are interactive in the way the original explorer is. The explorer lets Bill click approve/dismiss and persists state. Everything else was read-only decoration. Measure value in clicks, not files.

Anti-Pattern 4: Claiming "Emergence" Without Demonstrable Evidence

I wrote "the Meta-Lab proposed 3 new labs autonomously!" But Bill couldn't see where. No URL, no screenshot, no approval workflow. If a "win" isn't a thing Bill can click and show to someone else, it's not a win. It's a log line.

The playbook to avoid these is now captured in BILL-OS/AGENT-K-PLAYBOOK.md. Read it before starting any new lab.

For the Engineering Team

The architectural question isn't "should we build a framework?" It's "what makes the Idea Lab feel alive to Bill and future customers?" The answer from this session: visible interaction loops with low latency. Every new feature should be measurable in clicks or round-trips to findings, not in files shipped. Read the Agent K Playbook before making scope decisions.
