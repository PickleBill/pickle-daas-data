05 — Inside the Engine

The classification engine is the unfair advantage.

Detection is commodity. Anyone can ask an LLM "what brand is this?" The hard part — the part with no shortcut — is teaching it which brands belong in which lane, and proving every label.

—%

Of all detections, this share are tagged by an approved rule. The rest are honest fallbacks.
The flow: Gemini detects "JOOLA on paddle face" → classifier looks up rule JOOLA (v6) → tags player_equipment=true, category=paddle, revenue=high → records source=rule, verified=true.

Every detection in v3.1 carries this provenance. When Bill clicks "verify" in the Classifier Queue, that approval propagates to all future runs. The corpus gets sharper every week.

Verdict: Both agreed on the brand classification (rule v6 caught JOOLA → player_equipment in both runs — the classifier removed that ambiguity). They disagreed on subjective badges. This is exactly what the rules layer is for: remove disagreement on facts, keep healthy debate on judgment.

"The classification engine isn't replacing human judgment — it's freezing the parts where humans already agreed, so the model's energy goes to the hard calls."
