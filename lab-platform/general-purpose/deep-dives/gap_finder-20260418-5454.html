Gap: Data quality/confidence metadata for AI-extracted fields

Evidence

A 'quality' field exists (presumably video production quality), but there is NO confidence score, extraction certainty, or validation flag for the AI-extracted metadata itself (skills, shots, brands, badges). When gemini-2.5-flash-lite generated 4157/4283 records, no quality assurance metrics were recorded. Evidence: Three AI models used (gemini-2.5-flash-lite, gemini-tactical, gemini-2.5-flash) with vastly different counts (4157, 91, 35) but no model_confidence, extraction_error_rate, or per-field_reliability score.

Independent Validation

yes

Would change mind: This is legitimate. Show: (a) confidence scores for AI-extracted shot classifications (is that a dink or a drop?), (b) inter-rater reliability on badge assignments, (c) systematic error analysis. The absence of these is a real audit gap.

Blind spot: Doesn't distinguish *which* AI-extracted fields need confidence scores. A 'court location' extraction likely needs QC; a 'player username transcription' perhaps less so. Overgeneralizes the problem. Also silent on whether confidence data exists but is simply unreported.

Recommended Action

Identify which extracted fields (dominant_shot, brands, badges) are reliable vs. prone to misclassification. Prioritize revalidation of low-confidence records. Weight analysis by extraction reliability. Enable transparent reporting of AI extraction limitations.

Next Steps

  1. Identify which extracted fields (dominant_shot, brands, badges) are reliable vs. prone to misclassification. Prioritize revalidation of low-confidence records. Weight analysis by extraction reliability. Enable transparent reporting of AI extraction limitations.
  2. Validate with a focused sample. Test whether the signal holds at scale.
  3. If validated, build a proof-of-concept and share with the first potential user.
