05 — Inside the Engine

The classification layer is the unfair advantage.

Detection is a commodity. Any LLM can say "I see a JOOLA." The hard part — the part with no shortcut — is teaching it which lane that belongs in, proving every label, and version-controlling every decision.

—
Detections in

v6
Taxonomy
4 context flags + primary categories + revenue tier. Versioned, auditable, human-controlled.
—

Deterministic mappings reviewed and approved by a human before going to production.
—%
Rule coverage
Of all detections, this share are tagged by an approved rule. The rest are honest fallbacks.
The provenance chain: Gemini sees "JOOLA on paddle face" → classifier matches rule JOOLA (v6) → tags player_equipment=true, category=paddle, revenue=high → writes source=rule, verified=true, rule_version=6.

Every detection in v3.1 carries this full chain. When Bill clicks "verify" in the Classifier Queue, that approval propagates to all future runs retroactively. The corpus gets sharper every week without re-running anything.
