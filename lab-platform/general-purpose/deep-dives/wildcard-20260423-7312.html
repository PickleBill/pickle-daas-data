Evidence

Use your corpus to generate synthetic opponents with distinct shot patterns and play styles. Players train against AI that plays like specific pros or personas. Each training session generates new data that refines the AI. Players pay per session; your model gets better; better AI attracts more players. You own the only realistic pickleball sim in existence.

Independent Validation

partial

Would change mind: (a) A/B test: players training against synthetic AI vs. human sparring partners—does performance transfer? (b) Show synthetic opponents achieve realistic win rates against real players. (c) Prove shot sequences are learned, not memorized templates. (d) Measure if players beat the AI they trained against (overfitting risk). Current evidence is 'this is technically possible,' not 'this improves outcomes.'

Blind spot: Assumes simulation realism transfers to match play (court feel, pressure, fatigue, opponent adaptation). Synthetic opponents may exploit exploits rather than play sound pickleball. No evidence on whether training against AI prevents or enables bad habits. Ignores that learning from a machine playing deterministically may build fragile strategies.
