Evidence

Flip the corpus into a real-time talent marketplace where amateur players upload clips, get rated by the AI system, and connect directly with coaches, sponsors, and tournament organizers who need them. Each upload makes the matching algorithm better; each match validates the rating system; each validated rating attracts more serious players and scouts.

Devil's Advocate

high risk

The system may rate players high on technical metrics (touch, consistency) while missing coachability, work ethic, injury history, or market demand (e.g., local vs. national appeal). False positives in the AI ratings erode marketplace trust and adoption.

Independent Validation

partial

Would change mind: Evidence describes a plausible business model, not an AI research finding. Sufficient evidence would require: (a) prototype data showing amateur→coach conversion rates, (b) validation that AI ratings correlate with coach assessment, (c) evidence the corpus contains usable player identification/continuity across clips. Currently: zero empirical validation that the system works.

Blind spot: Assumes clip quality = player skill signal. Many confounds: camera angle, opponent strength, luck, single performance vs. consistency. No discussion of how AI distinguishes 'talented amateur' from 'good camera work.' Privacy/consent framework invisible—uploading sports footage involves minors and venue owners.
