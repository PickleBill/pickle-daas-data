Hidden Signals

Non-Obvious Patterns Most People Would Miss

Signal: Skill stability varies wildly by dimension

Some skills (like consistency) are measurable in 3 clips. Others (like creativity) need 15+. This means player profiles should show confidence intervals per skill, not just a number.

Signal: The 'kitchen mastery' paradox

Clips dominated by kitchen play have high quality scores but LOW viral potential. The plays that look best on social media are NOT the plays that coaches value most. Two different content strategies needed.

Signal: Brand detection is a proxy for player investment level

Clips with 3+ identifiable brands correlate with higher skill levels. Players who invest in branded gear tend to be more serious. This is a latent segmentation signal.

Signal: Shot sequencing reveals 'meta-game' evolution

The dink-dink-speedup pattern dominates winning sequences. Any player or AI that can predict the speedup moment has a decisive advantage. This is the pickleball equivalent of chess opening theory.

Signal: Moment density predicts rewatch value

Clips with >2.0 moments/second get rewatched. Clips below 0.5 moments/second are skipped. The density threshold should drive the auto-highlight detection algorithm.
